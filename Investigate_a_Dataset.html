Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset gathers data from 100,000 clinical arrangements in Brazil and is centered around whether patients appear for their arrangement. Various qualities about the patient are remembered for each column. See sections underneath

  • PatientID
  • AppointmentID
  • Gender
  • ScheduledDay - day the patient set up their appointment.
  • AppointmentDay - day the patient had the appointment.
  • Age
  • Neighborhood - indicates the location of the hospital.
  • Scholarship - indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família.
  • Hipertension
  • Diabetes
  • Alcoholism
  • Handcap
  • SMS_received - '0' if they did not receive and '1' if they did.
  • No-show: 'No' if the patient showed up to their appointment, and 'Yes' if they did not show up.
    • </ul>

    Conclusions¶

    • The majority of people usually show up to scheduled appointment.
    • The porportion of Females attending clinical appointments is higher than that of the males as the figures stands at 65% for females and 35 % for males.
    • Age of a patient is not a factor that affect the attendance of patients to appointments.
    • Minority of patients who did not show up for their appointments did Receive SMS.
    • Recieving SMS seems not to increase attendence.
